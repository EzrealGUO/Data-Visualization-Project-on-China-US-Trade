A Research on the Comparison and Correlation of the Economic and Trade
Between China and the US Based on the 2018 Trade Friction

By Ziyi Guo

Sino-American relationship is one of the most important and complex bilateral relations in the world today; China is the largest developing country while the United States is the largest developed country. The Trade Friction between the two super countries in 2018 drew the wide attention of the whole world and caused great affects to the global economy. What is the Global Economic Status of China and the US? How did their Regional Roles change in the past time? In what ways are the two countries related such tightly and where will both countries go in the future? The data will reveal the truth.

Global Economical Status


Global Economical Status represents the overall power of a nation in the global ecnomic. With the rapid development in the recent 40 years after Opening up and Reform, China is constantly rising in Global Economic Status, graduaally forming the new global landscape together with the consistent first global economy the Unitetd States and the European Countries. How was the Global Economical Status of China and the United States changing is the past years? Let's go to the details.

Economic Aggregate

As is shown in the story, the GDP of global major economies has changed so far since 1960 and the world pattern is developing dramatically. In addition to the visualization according to the data in 2018, the biggest three economies in the world are now the United States, the European Union, and China, which totally take up 61.60% of the global economy and the biggest economy is the United States, which takes up 23.88% of the world. It should be mentioned that the dotted lines in the visualization indicate the time and the name of the nation when the GDP of China went beyond the corresponding major economy.

From 1978 to 2018, the development of China and the US shows obvious periodicity. The distribution summarizes the process into four stages, including 1978 to 1991, 1992 to 2001, 2002 to 2007, and 2008 to 2018. Each Stage above represents a significant developing period of China and the US and indicates the core event that has taken place.

Specifically comparing the economy amount, the GDP based on the Parity Purchasing Power of China is only 10.69% of the US in 1980 but goes beyond the US in 2016 and is 1.05 times it in 2018; however, the GDP per capita of China is still 28.8% of the US at present. In other words, China has the economic aggregate close to the US, but there still appears to be a big gap in the per capita level.

Illustrating the inequality data of domestic top 1% income proportion from the World Inequality Database, it is apparently shown that the income inequality situation of the US is much more severe than China and other developed countries in the world and the attribution of income is more likely to skew to the rich group.

From the perspective of Industrial Composition, China is in the phase of industrial structure transformation and have enormous change in the Industry Add Value Share of World in Construction and Service (from 7.70% to 25.60% and 2.80% to 13.40%). In 2018, the industry add value of Service share of GDP has exceeded the proportion of Industry. Comparatively, the US has a decrease in the Industry and Construction add value share of world but constantly maintains the edge in the tertiary industry.

International Trade

As the major economies in the international trade, China has an enormous increase in goods trade with the export share of world from 0.78% in 1978 to 12.57% in 2018 and has been maintaining the status as the world's largest goods exporter since 2009, while the US is constantly consolidating the leading status in the global service trade. From the perspective of Trade Deficit, Trade Surplus and External Balance, China is mainly exporting goods and people are consuming service domestically at present, while the US appears the opposite.

International Direct Investment

Comparing the amount of the international investment, despite China is increasing international investment rapidly, there is still great gap between China and the US, and the Onward Investment Stock from China in 2018 is less than a quarter of it from the US. Looking at the development of the investment from the two countries, China has a huge negative growth in Onward Investment in 2017 because of the International Protectionism and domestic policy constrains, while the US consistently ranks the first in Investment Stock globally and has several times of mass foreign investment entering and capital reflow.
In addition to the visualization, up to 2018, the Onward Investment Stock towards developed countries from the US has reached 4.59 trillion dollars, while China has not ranked in the top 10 among all the investors. As for the Onward Investment Stock towards developing countries, China has reached an amount of 1.12 trillion dollars and is in the first place. Generally, the outbound investment from China is currently leaning towards developing countries.


Regional Economic Status


Based on the closer and closer global status of China and the US, more detailed exploration is going to the Regional Status of both countries. The region of China is defined as Asia-Southeast Asia and the region of the US is defined as the countries of NAFTA, including the US, Mexico and Canada. How are both countries influence the respective regions in economic and trade? Let's move on to the story.

Regional Importance

Analyzing the regional importance from the perspectives of GDP, Trade, Consumption, and International Investment, China is raising constantly in the GDP, Trade Volume and Consumption Share of Asia-Southeast Region, while the US is more stable in the respective region and has an overall downward tendency.

Regional Tendency

Regional Tendency indicates the proportion of a specific economic activity with the own region taking up the total amount of this activity. The Regional Tendency of China and the US both show features of down then up, indicating the regionalization tendency of the global trade.

Regional Influence Distribution

As for the influence of China and the US to all the regions all over the world. In 2018, there are totally 46 and 19 countries in the world regard China and the US as the biggest import source country and 54 and 46 countries regard China and the US as the biggest export destination country, among which a largest number of Asia-Southeast countries take China as the biggest trade partner while a great number of North America countries take the US as the biggest trade partner. Both has wide influence distribution globally, while China shows great influence over East-Southeast Asia and the US shows influence over NAFTA district.

Free Trade Agreement Network

Further exlporing the effective Free Trade Agreement realted to China and the US, there are totally 16 FTAs with China and 14 with the US. Despite of the difference of FTA amounts, the US has joined a deep FTA as NAFTA, while the East-Southeast Asia is comparatively loose with the complex FTAs between countries.

Sino-American Economy and Trade Correlations


Former US President Trump has repeatedly mentioned the concept of the China-US economic decoupling. Is that really available for both countries and even the whole world? Could China and the United States thoroughly eliminate the associations in Trade, Production, and Consumption with each other? Let's have a look at how China and the United States is tightly related to the other side.

Trade Correlations

China and the US are highly interdependent in trade. In 2018, the United States is the largest export destination and the third largest import source of goods from China, while China is the third largest export destination and the largest import source of the US.
For the dependence on China, the US mainly export agriculture and basic raw materials to China and import light industry products from China. The proportion of umbrellas in the US market made in China even take up 93% of the total.
For the dependence on the US, China mainly export aircrafts and weapons to the US and import aircrafts and explosives from the US.

Production Associations

China and the US are also highly interdependent in production. China is the fourth largest source and destination of overseas intermediate products of the United States, and the United States is the sixth largest source and the second largest destination of overseas intermediate products of China. The two countries have an obvious interconnection in the division of labor in the global value chain.

Consumption Correlations

Similar to Trade and Production, from the perspective of Consumption, China is the largest consumption source country and the third largest consumption destination of the US, while the US is the fourth largest consumption source country and the second largest consumption destination of China.

Basic Strength and Development Potential

In addition to the above macro economic indicators, we can also further focus on various subdivided indicators, and form a relatively comprehensive and specific understanding of the comparison of the economic and trade strength and development potential of China and the United States. In terms of basic strength comparison, infrastructure, military, energy, health and technology are selected to compare and judge several aspects concerning national production and life; for development potential, employment, education, finance and finance to see the economic responsibilities of current government and future development potential and space.
In terms of basic strength, China and the US has both advantages and disadvantages. The United States is relatively backward and invested in modern infrastructure, high in energy use and emissions, low healthcare efficiency and high cost. China still lags behind the United States in infrastructure stock, have room for strengthing military power, depend strongly on external energy, and have the potential to improve scientific technologies.
From the perspective of comprehensive potential development indices, there is still great room for the improvement in terms of labor production efficiency and education level for China. At the same time, there is a gap between the international status of the RMB and the US dollar, while the United States has greater risks in terms of government debt burden.








In terms of the Global Economic Status, China and the US are shown to be close in economic aggregate and trade scale, but different in development stages. In terms of International Investment, China has considerable developing potential, but still needs to continue catching up with the total amount. From the perspective of Regional Economic Status, both countries occupy a key status in their respective regions; in contrast, the importance of the US in NAFTA region is relatively stable but declining, while China’s importance in East-Southeast Asia region is increasing. On Economic and Trade Correlations, China and the US are largely closely linked in trade, production and consumption. As for the Basic Strength, both two countries have their own strengths and weaknesses, and there is great room for development in the future.

At present, the US, the EU and China all provide strong economic support for the region. As two big countries with similar strength and close associations, China and the United States are bound to share cooperation and competition in economic exchanges. It is always the right way to properly handle the economic and trade relations between the two countries, solve the frequent economic and trade frictions in all the developing stages and achieve reciprocal and win-win development.
